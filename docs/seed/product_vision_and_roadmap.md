# Product Vision and Roadmap

## Video Coaching Platform for Personal Trainers

---

# 1. Product Vision

## Long-Term Vision

The product will become a **video-first coaching platform for personal trainers**, enabling trainers to:

* review training videos efficiently
* provide structured coaching feedback
* monitor client progress over time
* strengthen client relationships
* scale their coaching business

The platform will combine:

* **video-based coaching**
* **trainer-centered AI assistance**
* **progress tracking**
* **trainer branding**

The system will **augment trainers rather than replace them**, positioning AI as a coaching assistant.

---

# 2. Product Philosophy

The design principles guiding development are:

### Trainer-first design

The platform exists to **enhance the trainer-client relationship**, not bypass it.

AI should assist the trainer rather than communicate final conclusions directly to the client.

---

### Video-first interaction

Video submissions are the core object in the system.

The primary workflow revolves around:

Client uploads video
→ trainer reviews
→ trainer sends feedback
→ client applies corrections

---

### Lightweight coaching loop

Feedback must be:

* quick to produce
* easy to consume
* repeatable

This encourages frequent interaction.

---

### Trainer ownership and branding

The platform should feel like **the trainer's coaching portal**, not a generic tool.

Trainer branding must appear in:

* client dashboards
* feedback pages
* email notifications

---

### Gradual intelligence

AI capabilities will be added gradually:

* first as insights
* later as trainer assistants
* never as autonomous coaches

---

# 3. Final Product State

The mature product will support a complete **video coaching ecosystem**.

---

# 3.1 Core Coaching Loop

The final workflow will be:

Client uploads video
↓
AI performs preliminary analysis
↓
Trainer receives structured insights
↓
Trainer reviews and adds coaching feedback
↓
Client receives enriched feedback
↓
Progress metrics update
↓
Client uploads next training video

This loop creates continuous engagement.

---

# 3.2 Core System Capabilities

The final system will include several major capability areas.

---

## Video Coaching

Clients can:

* upload training videos
* label exercises
* add notes

Trainers can:

* review videos
* comment on timestamps
* annotate movement
* provide written feedback
* send voice notes

---

## AI Coaching Assistant

AI provides support such as:

* rep detection
* movement segmentation
* bar path tracking
* technique flags
* velocity estimation
* fatigue detection

AI output will be **presented to trainers first**.

Trainers interpret and communicate feedback.

---

## Trainer Dashboard

Trainers will have tools to manage their coaching workload.

Capabilities include:

* pending submissions
* flagged technique issues
* client activity monitoring
* feedback response tracking

---

## Client Progress Tracking

The system will track performance over time.

Examples:

* squat depth consistency
* bar path improvement
* rep speed trends
* training frequency

Progress visualizations help clients see improvement.

---

## Trainer Branding

Each trainer can configure:

* business name
* logo
* headshot
* website
* bio

Branding appears across:

* client dashboards
* feedback reports
* email notifications

---

## Business Tools (Future)

Later versions may include:

* workout programming
* client subscriptions
* coaching plans
* group coaching
* automated client check-ins

These features support trainers' business growth.

# 4. Product Roadmap

Development will proceed through a sequence of MVPs.

Each MVP validates specific assumptions before introducing additional complexity.

---

# MVP1 – Video Feedback Platform

## Objective

Validate the core workflow:

Client video upload → trainer feedback.

---

## Features

Authentication

* trainer accounts
* client accounts

Trainer-client relationships

* invite clients
* manage client list

Video submissions

* upload training video
* select exercise
* add optional note

Trainer review

* video playback
* timestamp comments
* summary feedback
* optional voice note

Client feedback view

* watch video
* read comments
* listen to voice feedback

Notifications

* email notifications

Trainer branding

* display name
* business name
* logo
* headshot
* website link
* short bio

---

## Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if:

* clients regularly upload videos
* trainers consistently review submissions
* trainers prefer the workflow over messaging apps
* clients perceive feedback as valuable

---

# MVP2 – AI Instant Feedback

## Objective

Provide immediate value to clients and reduce trainer workload.

---

## Features

Automatic analysis after video upload:

* rep count
* movement segmentation
* simple technique flags

Examples:

* possible squat depth issue
* bar drifting forward
* lockout incomplete

Client receives lightweight feedback immediately.

Trainer receives deeper analysis report.

---

## Design Principles

AI must be framed as:

Preliminary analysis

Trainers remain responsible for coaching conclusions.

---

# MVP3 – Trainer AI Assistant

## Objective

Help trainers review videos faster.

---

## Features

AI generates draft coaching insights.

Examples:

* key timestamps
* suggested feedback cues
* technique observations

Trainer can:

* accept suggestions
* edit suggestions
* discard suggestions

AI becomes a **coaching assistant**.

---

# MVP4 – Progress Tracking

## Objective

---

## Features

Track metrics across submissions.

# 5. Development Strategy

The roadmap follows several principles.

---

## Validate workflow first

The most important question is:

Will trainers and clients adopt video coaching as a regular habit?

MVP1 answers this question.

---

## Add intelligence gradually

AI will be introduced in phases to avoid:

* incorrect coaching
* trainer displacement
* complex infrastructure

---

## Prioritize trainer experience

Trainer workflow efficiency determines adoption.

Key metric:

Time required to review a submission.

---

## Minimize friction for clients

Client upload must be:

* fast
* mobile-friendly
* simple

High friction will reduce submissions.

---

# 6. Risks

Several risks must be monitored.

---

## Client participation risk

Clients may not upload videos regularly.

Mitigation:

* simplify upload flow
* encourage trainers to request videos

---

## Trainer workload risk

Trainers may delay feedback.

Mitigation:

* clear pending submissions dashboard
* lightweight feedback tools

---

## AI trust risk

Clients may trust AI more than trainers.

Mitigation:

* AI insights routed through trainers

---

# 7. Success Indicators

Signs the product is working include:

* regular video submissions
* frequent trainer feedback
* repeated coaching loops
* improved trainer-client engagement
* trainers integrating the platform into their workflow

If these indicators appear during MVP1 and MVP2, the platform has strong potential to evolve into a full coaching system.

---

# 8. Long-Term Potential

If adoption is strong, the platform could expand into:

* remote coaching infrastructure
* data-driven training insights
* AI-assisted coaching
* trainer business platforms

The ultimate goal is to become the **primary interface between personal trainers and their clients for video-based coaching**.
